Course Objectives

  • 1.Introduce students to the principles of Design Thinking and the importance of challenging existing patterns for innovative problem-solving.
  • 2. Foster teamwork, positive thinking, and self-reflection through interactive and creative activities.
  • 3. Develop students' ability to visualize long-term goals, create user personas, and map user journeys for better understanding of user needs.
  • 4. Equip students with practical skills in ideation methods, mobile application design, and patent application processes.

Course Outcomes

  • CO1: Demonstrate an understanding of Design Thinking principles and the ability to identify and break existing patterns to develop innovative solutions.
  • CO2: Exhibit improved teamwork, positivity, and self-awareness through participation in various activities designed to enhance these skills.
  • CO3: Create detailed user personas, empathy maps, and journey maps to inform design decisions and improve user experiences.
  • CO4: Apply ideation techniques, design mobile applications using MIT App Inventor, and understand the process of designing and applying for patents.
  • CO5: Propose prototype solutions using appropriate tools.

Course Syllabus

This course covers the following topics in the design thinking process:

  • Introduction to Design Thinking
  • Empathy and User Research
  • Defining the Problem
  • Ideation Techniques
  • Prototyping Methods
  • Testing and Iteration

List of Experiments

  • Introduction of Design thinking and Break the pattern activity. 
  • Persona Identification: Creating personas of end-user to help in understanding the needs, goals, and pain points of the users.
  • Empathy map: mapping out what users say, think, feel, and do to gain deeper insights into their experiences.
  • Affinity mapping: organize and categorize data and insights collected during the empathize stage into meaningful patterns.
  • Pattern and Antipattern Identification: Identifying patterns and antipatterns helps in defining the problem by understanding common challenges and behaviors.
  • Theory of Prioritization (Impact of AI)
  • Prioritizing problems or features based on their impact, helping to define the key areas to focus on.
  • Visualization: Draw a poster to visualize ourselves in the next 4 year. Helps to implement the vision and goals set during the design thinking process.
  • Brainstorming: Generating a large number of ideas without judgment or criticism.
  • Be Positive (Write 20 Positive words impacting your life)
  • Ideation Methods- Mind Map, SCAMPER, SIT: - Using these ideation methods to brainstorm and generate a wide range of ideas.
  • Brainstorming and 3 Box Method: - Facilitating brainstorming sessions and using the 3 Box Method to organize and evaluate ideas.
  • Design a Mobile Application using MIT App Inventor: - Creating a prototype of a mobile application to test and iterate on the ideas generated.
  • Journey Map:- - Mapping out the user’s journey to identify touchpoints and test how the solution fits into the user’s life.
  • Design Patent Application:- - Working on a patent application can be seen as creating a prototype for protecting the innovative aspects of the idea.
  • Toss the Goal (Balloon Activity): - A fun, engaging activity that can symbolize the launch and implementation phase, encouraging active participation and teamwork.
  • Design an Autobiography: - This activity can be used to test how well the designed solution aligns with the user's journey and goals.

Grading Criteria

Your performance in this course will be evaluated based on the following components:

  • Participation: 20% (including discussions and interactive sessions)
  • Assignments: 40% (weekly assignments based on the course modules)
  • Project Work: 30% (a final project encompassing all stages of design thinking)
  • Final Quiz: 10% (to assess your understanding of the key concepts)
